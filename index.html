Top 4 Fighter jets of India

Bright up your knowledge with some of the basics about the indian airforce generation of the fighter jets . We promise that we will bring the best for you.

Dassault Mirage 2000

It is a French multirole fourth generation fighter made by Dassault Aviation. IAF purchased 49 Mirage 2000s in the 1980s. Among them, 42 are single-seaters and 7 are two-seaters.

Mikoyan Mig-29

A jet fighter aircraft manufactured by Mikoyan in the Soviet Union. IAF was the first international customer of the MiG-29. This particular aircraft was extensively used during the 1999 Kargil War in Kashmir by the IAF.

Mikoyan-Gurevich Mig-21

A supersonic fighter jet designed by Mikoyan-Gurevich Design Bureau in the Soviet Union. IAF is the third largest operator of the MiG 21s. Now IAF has 252 operational MiG-21s.

Sukhoi Su-30MKI

It is a multirole air superiority fighter developed in Russia by Sukhoi Design Bureau and built under license by India's HAL.

Do You Want To Join The Academy?

1
Through NDA:

Young boys who have passed class 12th are eligible to join AFA through NDA. They first spend the three years at NDA Khadakwasla, and then are shifted to AFA for Stage-I flying training.

2
Through NCC SE (Men only) :

As an Air Wing Senior Division ’C’ Certificate holder of the National Cadet Corps, you can apply to the Flying Branch of the Indian Air Force.

3
Through CDSE:

Male graduates can apply for the flying branch through CDSE Exam held twice a year by UPSC.

4
AFCAT:

Through AFCAT girls and boys can join the flying (SSC), Technical and Ground Duties Course. This exam is held twice a year in the month of Feb and August.

Eligibility Criteria — You Must Keep In Mind

Flying Branch Duties

Age limit: 19 to 23 years as on 01st January of the succeeding year.
Graduates (minimum 3 years degree course) in any discipline from a recognized university who have attained minimum 60% marks or B.E./B.Tech degree (4 year course) from a recognized university with minimum 60% marks in aggregate in all papers put together.

Technical Branch Duties

Age limit:18 to 28 years as on 01st January of the succeeding year.
Aeronautical Engineer (Electronics) AE (L): Candidates applying for AE (L) branch would be eligible.
Aeronautical Engineer (Mechanical) AE (M): Candidates applying for AE (M) branch would be eligible.

Ground Branch Duties

Age limit:20 to 23 years .
Accounts: Graduate degree in commerce (B.Com) (minimum 3 year degree course) with a minimum of 60% marks.
Education: Post graduate degree in any discipline with a minimum of 50% marks.
